What is Colour Management.........?

Colour management in simple terms is controlled conversion of colours across all colour producing devices.

Colour management in the printing industry involves a systematic approach to ensuring consistency and accuracy in colour reproduction across various printing devices and substrates. This process encompasses several key elements, including:

  • Calibration: Fine-tuning printing equipment to maintain consistent performance and output quality.
  • Linearization: Ensuring the relationship between input values and output values remains linear across the entire colour gamut to prevent colour shifts.
  • Characterization: Creating precise colour profiles for each printing device to map input colours to their corresponding output colours accurately.
  • Optimization: Fine-tuning printing processes and workflows to maximise efficiency and quality while minimising waste and production costs.
  • Standardization: Adhering to established industry standards and specifications for colour reproduction to ensure consistency and interoperability.

By implementing comprehensive colour management practices that encompass calibration, linearization, characterization, standardization, and optimization, printing businesses can achieve consistent, accurate, and high-quality colour reproduction across their entire workflow, from design to final output.
